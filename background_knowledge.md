Provisional background themes from the uploaded manuscript:

- The stratum corneum and viable epidermis impose major resistance to passive delivery of hydrophilic macromolecules and particulate systems.
- Microneedling can create transient aqueous microchannels that bypass superficial barrier layers.
- Conventional phosphatidylcholine/cholesterol liposomes may alter superficial stratum corneum lipid order or hydration, but are not assumed to cross intact human skin as intact vesicles.
- Post-application massage may influence topical delivery outcomes by changing formulation spreading, pore loading, or superficial tissue mechanics.
